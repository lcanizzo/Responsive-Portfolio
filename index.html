About Me

Hi I'm Lou. This is the first website I have written from scratch so please look around and poke everything for me. I love coding and enjoy projects that require me to learn new things. I have a B.A in Sociology from the University of Central Florida, where I ran a 300+ sample research study on the affects of motivation and barrier perception on participation in leisure time physical activity (L.T.P.A). Using SPSS and regression analysis, I found that there is a significant predictive inpact on participation in L.T.P.A dependent on the levels of motivation and perceived barriers. While working on this and other studies I found a passion for answering social problems and other questions seemingly unrelated to S.T.E.M by using programs, data, and statistical methods effectively.


Some of my favorite pass-times are: weight-lifting, exercising and hanging out with my dog, hiking, bird-watching, and exploring for the best restaurants.


On the other hand, we denounce with righteous indignation and dislike men who are so beguiled and demoralized by the charms of pleasure of the moment, so blinded by desire, that they cannot foresee the pain and trouble that are bound to ensue; and equal blame belongs to those who fail in their duty through weakness of will, which is the same as saying through shrinking from toil and pain. These cases are perfectly simple and easy to distinguish. In a free hour, when our power of choice is untrammelled and when nothing prevents our being able to do what we like best, every pleasure is to be welcomed and every pain avoided. But in certain circumstances and owing to the claims of duty or the obligations of business it will frequently occur that pleasures have to be repudiated and annoyances accepted. The wise man therefore always holds in these matters to this principle of selection: he rejects pleasures to secure other greater pleasures, or else he endures pains to avoid worse pains.
